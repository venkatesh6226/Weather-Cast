Satellites Used for Weather Forecasting

Satellites play a crucial role in weather forecasting by providing valuable data about the Earth's atmosphere and weather patterns. They help meteorologists monitor and track weather systems, gather information on temperature, humidity, cloud cover, wind patterns, and more. Here are some of the satellites commonly used for weather forecasting:

Geostationary Operational Environmental Satellites (GOES)

  • Operated by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) in the United States.
  • GOES satellites provide continuous monitoring of the weather in the Americas, capturing images and data at regular intervals.

Polar-orbiting Satellites

  • Satellites such as the NOAA-NASA Joint Polar Satellite System (JPSS) and Europe's MetOp orbit the Earth in a polar path, providing global coverage.
  • They collect data on atmospheric conditions, cloud cover, sea surface temperatures, and other variables that help improve weather forecasting models.

Himawari Satellites

  • Operated by the Japan Meteorological Agency (JMA).
  • The Himawari series of satellites provide high-resolution imagery and atmospheric data for the Western Pacific region, including East Asia and Australia.

Meteosat Satellites

  • Operated by the European Organization for the Exploitation of Meteorological Satellites (EUMETSAT).
  • The Meteosat series of satellites monitor weather conditions over Europe, Africa, and parts of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. They capture frequent images and data to support weather forecasting and monitoring.

Fengyun Satellites

  • Operated by the China Meteorological Administration (CMA).
  • The Fengyun series of satellites provide weather data for China and surrounding regions, contributing to regional and global weather forecasting efforts.
